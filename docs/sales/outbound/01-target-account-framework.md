# DORA Resilience Evidence Sprint — Outbound Kit (Part 1: Target Account Framework)

## How to use this kit

This is a ready-to-send target account framework. To execute this properly, the founder must still: 
1. Identify and verify the final 60 accounts.
2. Confirm each firm's DORA scope and the right contact.
3. Ensure all outreach complies with GDPR / ePrivacy (legitimate-interest B2B basis, no purchased lists, clear opt-out / unsubscribe in every email). 

*The example companies are illustrative ICP anchors only — not endorsements, customers, or a contact list.*

**The Product/Offer:** 
The sellable outcome is the "DORA Resilience Evidence Sprint" — a fixed-scope engagement delivering a "DORA pre-audit resilience evidence pack for ONE critical service." 
*   **Terms:** 5 business days, fixed scope, $2,500 total (50% upfront).
*   **Constraints:** Sanitized data only, NO production access, NO PII. 
*   **Scope limits:** It is decision-support / pre-audit preparation — NOT legal advice, NOT a DORA certification, NOT a guarantee of compliance, and does NOT replace threat-led penetration testing (TLPT) or auditor sign-off.

## 60-Account Target Framework

### Segment A: EU Payment / E-Money Institutions (≈ 35 Accounts)

**1. ICP criteria**
*   **Qualifiers:** EU-authorized payment institution (PI) or e-money institution (EMI). Size: 50–2,000 employees. Cloud-native architecture (AWS/GCP/Azure, API-first flows). High dependency on ICT third-party service providers (gateways, BaaS, cloud hosts). DORA-pressure triggers: imminent internal audits, board-level reporting needs, NCA readiness reviews, critical service mapping gaps.
*   **Disqualifiers:** Micro-enterprises exempt from core DORA requirements; fully on-premises legacy infrastructure; no regulated EU operational footprint; demands production testing or live PII access.

**2. Where to source the real list**
*   **Official Registers:** EBA payment-institution / e-money register; national competent-authority registers including BaFin (DE), ACPR / REGAFI (FR), Central Bank of Ireland, Bank of Lithuania, De Nederlandsche Bank, and CSSF (LU).
*   **Crunchbase Filters:** `Industries: Payments, FinTech, Financial Services | Headquarters Location: European Union | Number of Employees: 51-2000 | Operating Status: Active`
*   **LinkedIn Sales Navigator String 1:** `Title: ("Head of Operational Resilience" OR "CISO" OR "Head of ICT Risk") AND Industry: ("Financial Services" OR "Financial Technology") AND Geography: ("European Union") AND Headcount: (51-2000)`
*   **LinkedIn Sales Navigator String 2:** `Title: ("DORA Programme Lead" OR "COO") AND Keyword: ("EMI" OR "payment institution" OR "PSD2") AND Geography: ("European Union")`

**3. Illustrative ICP Anchors (Real EU PIs/EMIs/Fintechs)**
*   **Wise Europe SA (BE):** Belgian-regulated institution supporting massive cross-border money movement and EU payment operations.
*   **Mollie B.V. (NL):** DNB-authorized payment institution heavily reliant on continuous availability and modern cloud infrastructure.
*   **Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd. (IE):** Irish-regulated EU entity providing business-critical card and platform payment services.
*   **Mangopay S.A. (LU):** CSSF-authorized e-money institution heavily integrated into marketplace payment flows and third-party APIs.
*   **Qonto / Olinda SAS (FR):** ACPR-regulated business finance fintech with a cloud-first architecture and scaling dependencies.
*   **Pleo Financial Services A/S (DK):** Danish e-money institution running a pan-European corporate spend management platform.
*   **Treezor SAS (FR):** ACPR-authorized e-money institution providing core banking-as-a-service and card issuing infrastructure.

### Segment B: MiCA Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs) (≈ 15 Accounts)

**1. ICP criteria**
*   **Qualifiers:** Registered Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) or exchanges actively transitioning to MiCA-regulated CASP status in the EU. Size: 50–1,000+ employees. Cloud-native. Strong ICT third-party dependency (node providers, custody tech, cloud, KYC vendors). DORA-pressure triggers: overlapping MiCA/DORA authorization scrutiny from national regulators.
*   **Disqualifiers:** Pure DeFi protocols without an accountable EU legal entity; offshore-only platforms; token issuers with no active CASP infrastructure; demands access to live trading systems or private keys.

**2. Where to source the real list**
*   **Official Registers:** ESMA MiCA CASP register; national registers including AMF (FR) PSAN register, BaFin (DE) crypto custody register, DNB (NL) crypto provider register, Central Bank of Ireland VASP register.
*   **Crunchbase Filters:** `Industries: Cryptocurrency, Blockchain, Digital Assets | Headquarters Location: European Union | Number of Employees: 51-1000`
*   **LinkedIn Sales Navigator String 1:** `Title: ("CISO" OR "Head of Risk" OR "Head of Compliance") AND Industry: ("Blockchain Services" OR "Financial Services") AND Geography: ("European Union") AND Keyword: ("MiCA" OR "DORA" OR "CASP")`
*   **LinkedIn Sales Navigator String 2:** `Title: ("DORA Programme Lead" OR "CTO") AND Company Keyword: ("Crypto exchange" OR "Custody") AND Geography: ("Europe")`

**3. Illustrative ICP Anchors (Real EU-operating CASPs)**
*   **Bitpanda (AT):** Well-known EU crypto broker with active MiCA / CASP licensing activity and deep regulated EU operations.
*   **Bitstamp Europe S.A. (LU):** Luxembourg-based crypto exchange with a long-standing EU regulatory footprint preparing for MiCA.
*   **Coinbase Europe (IE):** Major EU-operating platform leveraging Irish and national registrations for its MiCA authorization pathway.

*   **Bitvavo B.V. (NL):** Dutch-registered crypto exchange managing high-volume trading and custody infrastructure scaling under DORA/MiCA.
*   **Coinhouse (FR):** AMF-registered PSAN and crypto broker actively dealing with French regulator scrutiny and CASP transition.

*Reminder: These are illustrative examples only and must be independently verified for current DORA scope and contact appropriateness.*
