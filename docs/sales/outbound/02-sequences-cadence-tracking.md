## 2. Cadence Calendar & Channel Mix

| Day | Channel | Action | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Email | Send Touch 1 | Personalize with {institution}, {firm}, {critical_service}, jurisdiction, or a relevant trigger. |
| Day 2 | LinkedIn | View profile or send connection request | Keep it non-pitchy: “Relevant to DORA resilience evidence — would be good to connect.” |
| Day 3 | Email | Send Touch 2 | Explain the Sprint mechanics and terms; do not attach files unless requested. |
| Day 5 | LinkedIn | Light engagement | Like or comment only if genuinely relevant; avoid generic “checking in” messages. |
| Day 8 | Email | Send Touch 3 | Add the DORA angle/proof-of-thinking; use a soft CTA. |
| Day 12 | LinkedIn | Short message if connected | One line: “Sent a note on DORA evidence for {critical_service}; worth comparing?” |
| Day 15 | Email | Send Touch 4 break-up | Close politely and make the opt-out easy. |
| Day 21 | CRM cleanup | Update status and next action | Stop if no engagement; revisit only on a real trigger or agreed timing. |

**Channel mix notes**

- Email is the primary channel; LinkedIn is for context, light familiarity, and respectful reinforcement.
- For a solo founder, keep volume manageable: 15–25 highly researched accounts per week is more realistic than broad blasting.
- Send Tuesday–Thursday in the recipient’s local time zone, roughly 08:00–10:00 or 14:00–16:00. Avoid late-night, weekend, and holiday sends.
- Handle replies within one business day. If positive, propose a 20-minute scoping call; if they ask for information, send a concise one-page scope note and input checklist.
- Stop immediately on any opt-out, unsubscribe, or negative privacy signal. Do not continue via LinkedIn after an email opt-out.
- Do not over-contact: four emails plus light LinkedIn activity over about 15 days is enough unless the prospect engages.

## 5. 20-Minute Scoping-Call Script

### 0:00–2:00 — Opening: rapport + permission

“Thanks for taking the time, {first_name}. I’ll keep this to 20 minutes. My goal is to understand whether there is ONE critical service where a fixed-scope DORA pre-audit evidence pack would be useful, and whether FaultRay is a fit. If not, I’ll say so. Does that work?”

“Quick scope reminder: this is decision-support / pre-audit prep — NOT legal advice, NOT a DORA certification, NOT a compliance guarantee, and it does not replace TLPT or auditor sign-off.”

### 2:00–8:00 — Discovery: identify the ONE critical service and DORA pressure

1. “What prompted the conversation now — DORA readiness, internal audit, board reporting, regulator expectations, MiCA/CASP scope, a client request, or something else?”
2. “Which ONE critical service would be most useful to examine first: {critical_service}, another customer-facing service, or a key internal dependency?”
3. “What are the main ICT dependencies behind that service — cloud, payment processor, wallet/custody stack, KYC/AML provider, APIs, data stores, monitoring, vendors, or incident response?”
4. “What evidence already exists, and what feels thin — dependency maps, incident history, BIA, exit plans, recovery assumptions, test results, third-party controls, or management reporting?”

### 8:00–11:00 — Scope fit check

Confirm:

- “Can we keep this to ONE critical service?”
- “Can your team provide sanitized inputs only?”
- “No production access and NO PII — is that workable?”
- “Is someone available for a short kickoff, async clarifications, and a final debrief within 5 business days?”
- “What would make the evidence pack useful internally: audit prep, management discussion, advisor handoff, remediation planning, or prioritization?”

If the need is live testing, legal interpretation, multi-service mapping, certification, or TLPT replacement, say:

“That is outside this Sprint; I do not want to overstate what FaultRay can do.”

### 11:00–14:00 — 2-minute explanation of the Sprint

“The offer is the "DORA Resilience Evidence Sprint" — fixed-scope, delivers a "DORA pre-audit resilience evidence pack for ONE critical service." Terms are fixed: 5 business days, fixed scope, $2,500 total (50% upfront), sanitized data only, NO production access, NO PII.”

“Typical flow: Day 0/1 we confirm the service, inputs, and assumptions. Days 2–3 FaultRay runs resilience scenarios against the dependency model and failure hypotheses. Day 4 we assemble findings, evidence gaps, and next-action prompts. Day 5 we deliver the pack and run a short debrief.”

“The deliverable is practical: service context, dependency assumptions, scenario notes, failure-mode observations, gaps/questions, and suggested next actions for internal, advisory, or pre-audit discussions.”

### 14:00–18:00 — Clear close

“Based on what you shared, it sounds like {critical_service} is / is not a good Sprint candidate because {reason}. If we proceed, I’d scope it as: ONE critical service, 5 business days, fixed scope, $2,500 total, 50% upfront, sanitized data only, NO production access, and NO PII.”

“Would you like me to send a one-page scope confirmation and the sanitized input checklist?”

If yes:

- “Who should receive the scope note and invoice?”
- “What kickoff date works?”
- “Who can provide the sanitized diagrams, dependency list, and current resilience assumptions?”
- “I’ll send the scope confirmation, 50% upfront invoice, and input checklist today. Once the upfront payment and inputs are in, the 5-business-day Sprint starts.”

### 18:00–20:00 — If not ready, not a fit, or Segment C partner path

If not ready:

“No problem. Should I close the loop, send the one-page overview, or revisit once around {revisit_month}?”

If not a fit:

“Based on what you need, I do not think the fixed-scope Sprint is the right tool. You may need broader advisory, live testing, TLPT support, legal advice, or auditor-led work.”

For Segment C partner/referrer calls:

“For a partner model, your team owns the client relationship, DORA interpretation, and advisory recommendations. FaultRay sits behind the scenes as the resilience evidence engine for ONE client critical service, under a referral or white-label structure we agree separately.”
