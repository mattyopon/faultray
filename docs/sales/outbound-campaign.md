# DORA Resilience Evidence Sprint — Outbound Campaign

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP

FaultRay is best suited for cloud-native EU financial institutions in DORA scope that need practical, service-level resilience evidence before an internal audit, supervisory conversation, board risk review, or external advisory review.

Best-fit end buyers are:

- Licensed EU payment institutions and e-money institutions.
- API-first payment, wallet, ledger, merchant acquiring, payment initiation, account information, card, or embedded finance providers.
- Regulated fintechs with meaningful ICT third-party dependencies: cloud platforms, managed databases, payment processors, identity providers, observability tools, KYC/KYB vendors, fraud tooling, core banking platforms, card processors, or outsourcing partners.
- Teams that already have DORA policies, control mappings, risk registers, or advisor support, but lack a focused evidence pack for one named critical or important service.

Adjacent fit:

- Crypto-asset service providers in DORA scope, especially firms preparing for or operating under MiCA authorization.
- Regulated fintech infrastructure providers where disruption to one service could affect customer access, payments, custody, settlement, trading, or account operations.
- DORA advisory boutiques and ICT risk consultancies that need a narrow technical evidence module for clients.

### Problem Owners

The buyer is usually the person responsible for turning DORA expectations into operational evidence.

Primary owners:

- Operational Resilience Lead
- ICT Risk Manager / Head of ICT Risk
- CISO / Security Lead
- Head of Compliance
- Head of Risk
- COO / Operations Lead

Technical contributors:

- CTO / VP Engineering
- Head of Platform / Infrastructure
- SRE / DevOps Lead
- Product owner for the critical service

Influencers:

- Internal audit
- External DORA advisor
- Legal or compliance counsel
- Board risk committee stakeholders
- Third-party risk or outsourcing lead

### Company Stage and Size Signals

Prioritize firms large enough to face real DORA pressure, but lean enough that a fixed-scope, founder-led sprint is useful.

Good-fit signals:

- Regulated EU PI, EMI, CASP, or adjacent financial entity.
- Cloud-native or hybrid-cloud operating model.
- Live customer-facing critical services.
- Multiple ICT third-party dependencies.
- A named service that can be scoped in one call.
- Public hiring for DORA, ICT risk, operational resilience, cloud security, SRE, platform engineering, compliance, or internal audit.
- Recent license activity, new market entry, product launch, platform migration, funding, acquisition, or outsourcing change.
- Compliance or risk team has policies and registers, but engineering evidence is fragmented.

Indicative stage:

- Scale-up to mid-market fintech.
- Mature enough to have risk, compliance, and engineering owners.
- Not so large that a five-day external sprint would be swallowed by enterprise procurement unless sponsored by a specific service team.

### Trigger Events: Why Now

DORA has applied since January 2025, so the need has shifted from readiness planning to evidence quality.

High-priority triggers:

- Upcoming internal audit, external audit, board risk review, or supervisory discussion.
- Preparation for supervisory testing or resilience evidence requests.
- Review of a critical or important function.
- New or changed ICT third-party dependency.
- Cloud migration, payment processor change, core platform change, identity provider change, custody provider change, or observability redesign.
- Recent outage, near miss, incident, post-mortem, or risk acceptance decision.
- MiCA/CASP authorization activity.
- Updating the ICT third-party register or register of information.
- Internal pressure to show more than policy language for one critical service.

### Offer Fit

The DORA Resilience Evidence Sprint is deliberately narrow:

- One critical service.
- Five business days.
- Fixed scope.
- $2,500 total.
- 50% upfront.
- Sanitized data only.
- No production access.
- No PII.
- No credentials, secrets, or customer records.

The outcome is a DORA pre-audit resilience evidence pack for one critical service. It is decision-support and pre-audit preparation, not legal advice, not a DORA certification, not a guarantee of compliance, and not a replacement for TLPT or auditor sign-off.

## 2. 60-Prospect Target Framework

This is a sourcing framework for building a 60-account list. It is not a list of named firms. Each account should be verified individually for regulatory status, service fit, likely DORA pressure, relevant contact, and outreach appropriateness.

Recommended split:

- Segment A: 35 accounts — EU payment / e-money institutions
- Segment B: 15 accounts — CASPs in DORA scope
- Segment C: 10 accounts — DORA advisory boutiques / consultancies

### Segment A: EU Payment / E-Money Institutions — 35 Accounts

#### Who They Are

Licensed EU payment institutions and e-money institutions operating customer-facing financial services, especially:

- Account-to-account payments
- Payment initiation services
- Account information services
- E-money wallets
- Merchant payments
- Card issuing or acquiring support
- Ledger and reconciliation services
- Embedded finance infrastructure
- Fraud or risk decisioning tied to payment availability
- Customer onboarding where disruption blocks access to regulated services

These are the primary end buyers.

#### Where to Source Names

Use public and regulatory sources first:

- National competent authority registers for payment institutions and e-money institutions.
- EBA payment and e-money institution register sources and related national register links.
- Public authorization, passporting, or license pages from national regulators.
- Company websites describing regulated EU payment or e-money services.
- Public job postings for DORA, ICT risk, operational resilience, compliance, SRE, cloud security, platform engineering, or internal audit.
- Industry association directories for EU payments, e-money, fintech, or regulated financial services.
- Conference agendas and webinar speaker lists focused on DORA, fintech compliance, payments risk, or operational resilience.

#### Selection Criteria

Prioritize accounts with:

- Confirmed EU PI or EMI authorization.
- Cloud-native or API-first operating model.
- Clear customer-facing critical services.
- Meaningful ICT third-party dependencies.
- Public evidence of growth, licensing activity, infrastructure change, market expansion, or hiring.
- A likely internal owner in ICT risk, operational resilience, security, compliance, or engineering.
- A service that can be scoped without production access or PII.

Strong first-service candidates:

- Payment authorization flow
- Payment initiation API
- Customer wallet ledger
- Merchant settlement flow
- Card transaction processing path
- Customer onboarding path
- Reconciliation or ledger posting service

#### Disqualifiers

Avoid accounts that:

- Are inactive, suspended, dormant, or in liquidation.
- Are agents or distributors without direct operational responsibility.
- Have no EU-regulated entity or no clear EU authorization.
- Require production access, credentials, secrets, PII, or live system testing.
- Need legal advice, formal compliance sign-off, certification, or TLPT.
- Have no identifiable critical service.
- Are so large and mature that a small fixed-scope sprint is unlikely to be useful unless a specific team-level need exists.

#### One-Line Value Prop

Turn one critical payment or e-money service into a DORA-oriented pre-audit resilience evidence pack in five business days, using sanitized data only.

## 4. Objection Handling

| Objection | Honest Response |
|---|---|
| “We already have a Big-4 audit or external DORA advisor.” | Good. FaultRay is not meant to replace enterprise-wide advisory or audit work; it produces a narrow technical evidence pack for one critical service. If your existing work already covers service-level resilience evidence in enough detail, the Sprint may not be needed. |
| “No production access means this will not be useful.” | Correct that it will not validate live production behavior, and it is not a chaos test or penetration test. Its value is pre-audit decision-support: mapping dependencies, modeling plausible disruption scenarios, and identifying evidence gaps from sanitized artifacts. |
| “$2,500 seems too low to trust.” | The price is low because the scope is intentionally constrained: one service, five business days, no production access, and a defined deliverable. It is not an enterprise DORA program or formal audit. |
| “$2,500 seems high for a short sprint.” | It is too high if there is no near-term audit pressure or no specific critical service to review. It makes sense only when a focused evidence pack would save internal time or clarify gaps before a review. |
| “We handle resilience internally.” | That may be the right approach. The Sprint is useful when an internal team wants an outside structured pass on one service; it is unnecessary if your team already has review-ready evidence. |
| “Is this legal or compliance advice?” | No. FaultRay provides technical and operational decision-support for pre-audit preparation. Legal interpretation, compliance conclusions, and sign-off remain with counsel, compliance, auditors, or supervisors. |
| “We have data security concerns.” | The engagement is designed around data minimization: sanitized data only, no PII, no credentials, no secrets, and no production access. If an artifact cannot be safely sanitized, it should not be shared. |
| “Does this replace TLPT, audit sign-off, or DORA certification?” | No. The Sprint is not a DORA certification, not a guarantee of compliance, and not a replacement for TLPT, live resilience testing, auditor sign-off, or supervisory judgment. It is a pre-audit evidence preparation sprint for one service. |

## 5. 20-Minute Scoping-Call Script

### 0:00–2:00 — Opening and Permission

“Thanks for taking the time, {first_name}. I’ll keep this to 20 minutes.

The goal is to determine whether one critical service at {institution} is a fit for a fixed-scope DORA Resilience Evidence Sprint. If it is not a fit, I’ll say so directly.

Just to set the boundary upfront: this is decision-support and pre-audit preparation. It is not legal advice, not a DORA certification, not a guarantee of compliance, and not a replacement for TLPT or auditor sign-off.

Does that agenda work?”

### 2:00–10:00 — Discovery Questions

1. **Identify the one service**  
   “If you had to pick one critical or important service for a DORA evidence review, which would it be — for example payment authorization, wallet ledger, onboarding, customer access, custody withdrawal, API availability, or another service?”

2. **Understand the DORA pressure**  
   “What is driving the timing: internal audit, external audit, board review, supervisory testing preparation, a critical-service review, a recent incident, or a new ICT third-party dependency?”

3. **Check current evidence**  
   “What evidence already exists for that service — architecture diagrams, dependency maps, runbooks, recovery targets, incident notes, test results, third-party register entries, or risk assessments?”

4. **Find the gap**  
   “Where do you feel least confident today: dependency visibility, third-party failure assumptions, scenario coverage, recovery evidence, incident response handoffs, or the audit narrative?”

### 10:00–12:00 — Two-Minute Explanation of the Sprint

“The Sprint is a five-business-day, fixed-scope engagement for one critical service.

You provide sanitized artifacts only: service overview, architecture or dependency diagrams, runbook excerpts, recovery objectives, known risks, incident notes if safe to share, and relevant ICT third-party context.

FaultRay uses that material to model plausible disruption scenarios and organize the findings into a DORA pre-audit resilience evidence pack.

You walk away with:

- A concise service and dependency map.
- A scenario matrix for plausible ICT disruptions.
- An assumptions log showing what was known, unknown, and inferred.
- A gap register for missing or weak evidence.
- A prioritized remediation backlog.
- Audit-prep questions for internal review.
- A final walkthrough of what the evidence does and does not support.

The output helps prepare for review; it does not certify compliance or replace legal, audit, supervisory, or TLPT work.”

### 12:00–17:00 — Fit Check and Scope Confirmation

Confirm the engagement can stay inside the fixed scope:

- “Can we keep this to one critical service?”
- “Can the required artifacts be sanitized?”
- “Can we avoid all PII, secrets, credentials, customer records, and production access?”
- “Is there an internal owner available during the five business days?”
- “Who needs the output: ICT risk, compliance, internal audit, external advisor, engineering, board risk, or another stakeholder?”
- “What would make the evidence pack useful by the end of day five?”

If it is not a fit:

“Based on what you’ve said, I would not recommend starting the Sprint right now. It sounds like you need {legal advice / full audit / live testing / enterprise-wide assessment / more internal scoping} first.”

### 17:00–20:00 — Close and Next Step

If there is a fit:

“Based on this, {critical_service} sounds like a reasonable candidate.

The terms are fixed:

- Five business days.
- One critical service.
- $2,500 total.

Should I send that scope to you, and who else should review it?”
